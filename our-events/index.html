Nukkad Natak

The best way to understand something is visualizing and what could be better than visualization and live interaction. This event was about importance of cleanliness. The act consisted of two major scenes,

  • An act of a household in a rural area and their quarrels for SHAUCHALAYA KI AAVASHAYAKTA (Need of Toilets).
  • Another act was about people in urban cities and their careless ATTITUDE TOWARDS CLEANLINESS
  • Followed by public interaction and a cleanliness drive.

Plantation Drive

With the adverse climate change, we are facing, it is everyones responsibility to do their part to protect our mother earth. So, we tried to do our part by organising a Plantation Drive. We wanted to make our plantation drive affective. Keeping this in mind, our society planted CHAMPA plant because of its characteristics. We made the youngsters to plant more plants and take care of them regularly. Around 100+ students joined our drive and help us plant saplings in and around our college which was also joined by residents and passer-bys, helping us plant 50+ saplings within a single day.

Cleanliness Drive

Cleanliness is most important for physical well-being and a healthy environment. It is essential for everyone to learn about cleanliness, hygiene, sanitation and the various diseases that are caused due to poor hygienic conditions. Seeing the current situations our society decided to take an initiative to clean the areas near to our society and did well in it. In the posh region of sector 23 & sector 22 we tried our best to collect all the dry garbage and through the efforts of the volunteers we achieved the target of collecting 50 kg+ of dry garbage.

School Visit

In the first week of March, team EWB made a visit to the school in saraswatienclave. We deliver a session on the topic of digital study and E-learning. In todays era there are manyresources for the students to learn online and with the use of devices such as projectors and pointerteacher can explain better to the students and hence resulting in better results. With this vision in ourmind we deliver a session of around 1 hour to the students as well as their parents talking about theresources present on the internet and how to access them. Session was interactive many parentsactively participated in the session and asked questions regarding the same.

Dental Camp

On march 12,2020 Dental camp is held at tigra village located in sector 57,gurugram. In most of the remote and village areas, people stick to the traditional style i.e. using datun(alittle stem) for cleaning teeth instead of using toothbrushes. It was found that people who were usingdatun had more issues in their teeth as compare to those who are using toothbrushes. Dental camp isfree for everyone. Many people came for check up. Dr. Ruchi Sharma held the dental camp to createawareness about teeth and the use of modern style and toothbrushes instead of traditional style.
